    Masters year

    A radial velocity study of the cataclysmic variable LAMOST J035913.61+405035.0

    This research project involved using spectroscopic observations with the Intermediate Dispersion Spectrograph on the 2.5-m Isaac Newton Telescope in La Palma to determine the component masses of the eclipsing CV LAMOST J035913.61+405035.0. The data were reduced and analysed using PYTHON.

    Consciousness

    This article was part of the assessment for the astrobiology module. What is consciousness? Why does it exist? How much can we know about consciousness? These questions are explored briefly with considerations of professional opinions on the matter.

    Aliens

    This essay is a response to the statement "Aliens must have almost certainly visited our Solar System". The origin of life on Earth is discussed, as well as the likelyhood of life existing outside of our own Solar System. The Fermi problem and Drake equation are both assessed.

    Planetary nebulae

    What is the role of binary stars in the formation of planetary nebulae? This "News & Views" style report gives an overview of this topic, outlining the formation process of planetary nebulae from binary star systems.

    Eclipsing binaries

    What do eclipsing binaries tell us about star formation? Eclipsing binaries are extremely useful probes for determining stellar properties, and therefore study their evolution.

    Reflecting telescopes

    This "News & Views" style article discusses the Crossley Reflector at the Lick observatory, from the point of view of people in the year 1899.

    Ancient Egyptian astronomy

    The "Ancient Egyptians" built great pyramids, sculptures and temples. Thousands of years earlier than this, they were some of the first astronomers on the Earth.

    The galaxy luminosity function

    A coding project to determine the cosmological parameters of a model universe by fitting a Schecter function to a luminosity function. The galaxy sample was created at random, with the cosmological parameters not representing real values.



    La Palma

    The skyWATCH all-sky camera

    An all-sky camera designed to track targets across the entire sky and monitor weather patterns for a low cost. The motions of targets, standards and the Moon were tracked and projected.

    European Near Earth Asteroid Research (EURONEAR)

    Observational project where the Near Earth Asteroid (NEA) 2023 DZ² was discovered with the 2.5-m Isaac Newton Telescope in La Palma. The discovery led to a paper and addition to the EURONEAR and NASA NEA database.

    Support astronomy at the ING

    Support astronomer on the 2.5-m Isaac Newton Telescope working for the Isaac Newton Group of Telescopes. Roles included training visiting observers to use the telescope, performing service observations, and calibrating and setting up the Wide Field Camera and Intermediate Dispersion Spectrograph instruments.



    Third Year

    Determining the inclination and radius of the accretion disc in the cataclysmic variable V1315 Aquilae

    Photometric observations from the 0.5-m pt5m telescope in La Palma were used to determine the inclination and radius of the accretion disc in an eclipsing cataclysmic variable. Observations were taken in two filters to estimate the temperature gradient in the disc.

    Tremor analysis and its uses in the detection of Parkinson's disease

    A simple circuit was built and tested with the purpose of detecting miniscule tremors in the fingers of patients with suspected Parkinson's disease. These tremors were too small to be detectable by-eye, but the frequencies detected by the circuit could be compared with literature values.

    The Saha equation

    Coding project and report which investigated the Saha equation and its uses in probing and modelling stellar atmospheres such as that of the Sun.
