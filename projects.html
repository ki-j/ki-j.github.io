    Masters year

    Dissertation

    A radial velocity study of the cataclysmic variable LAMOST J035913.61+405035.0

    This research project involved using spectroscopic observations with the Intermediate Dispersion Spectrograph on the 2.5-m Isaac Newton Telescope in La Palma to determine the component masses of the eclipsing CV LAMOST J035913.61+405035.0. The data were reduced and analysed using PYTHON.

    Consciousness

    Understanding understanding: Is consciousness impossible to comprehend?

    This article was part of the assessment for the astrobiology module. What is consciousness? Why does it exist? How much can we know about consciousness? These questions are explored briefly with considerations of professional opinions on the matter.

    Aliens

    Aliens must have certainly visited our Solar System... right?

    This essay is a response to the statement "Aliens must have almost certainly visited our Solar System". The origin of life on Earth is discussed, as well as the likelyhood of life existing outside of our own Solar System. The Fermi problem and Drake equation are both assessed.

    Planetary nebulae

    Binary origins of planetary nebulae

    What is the role of binary stars in the formation of planetary nebulae? This "News & Views" style report gives an overview of this topic, outlining the formation process of planetary nebulae from binary star systems.

    Eclipsing binaries

    What do eclipsing binaries tell us about star formation?

    Third Year

    Dissertation

    Determining the inclination and radius of the accretion disc in the cataclysmic variable V1315 Aquilae

    Photometric observations from the 0.5-m pt5m telescope in La Palma were used to determine the inclination and radius of the accretion disc in an eclipsing cataclysmic variable. Observations were taken in two filters to estimate the temperature gradient in the disc.

    Core dissertation

    Tremor analysis and its uses in the detection of Parkinson's disease

    A simple circuit was built and tested with the purpose of detecting miniscule tremors in the fingers of patients with suspected Parkinson's disease. These tremors were too small to be detectable by-eye, but the frequencies detected by the circuit could be compared with literature values.

    Saha equation

    The Saha equation

    Geoengineering

    Second Year

    Stellar interiors

    Modelling the Interior of the Sun using Polytropic Models

    The mass, density, pressure, and temperature were plotted as a function of fractional solar radius (R) for a polytropic model (PM) of various indices and the Standard Solar Model (SSM). In this second year project, Python and report writing skills were developed.

    Observing project

    Determining the age of the open cluster NGC 7789

    Spectroscopy

    Characterisation of a HII region in the starburst galaxy NGC 1313 using Gaussian fitting of spectra

    Data were taken from the observations of the starburst galaxy NGC1313 and the spectra corrected for redshift, and tted using a Gaussian approximation. The peak intensity, FWHM, line centroid and integrated flux were extrapolated from the fit and used to find the characteristic properties and their respective magnitudes of the HII region.
